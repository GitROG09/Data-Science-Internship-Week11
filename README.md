# Customer Segment Profiles

## Overview
Customers were segmented using K-Means clustering after feature scaling.  
Three distinct customer segments were identified based on behavioral and numerical patterns.

---

## Segment 0 – Premium Spenders
**Size:** ~25% of customers

### Characteristics
- High spending behavior
- Lower churn probability
- Stable usage patterns
- Less price-sensitive

### Business Interpretation
These customers generate the highest revenue and show strong brand loyalty.

### Recommended Actions
- Loyalty rewards and premium plans
- Early access to new features
- Personalized engagement campaigns

---

## Segment 1 – Budget Conscious Customers
**Size:** ~30% of customers

### Characteristics
- Low to moderate spending
- High churn risk
- Price-sensitive behavior

### Business Interpretation
These customers are highly cost-aware and likely to leave if better offers exist.

### Recommended Actions
- Discount-based retention strategies
- Flexible pricing plans
- Promotional offers during churn-risk periods

---

## Segment 2 – Young Professionals
**Size:** ~20% of customers

### Characteristics
- Moderate spending
- Growth potential
- Medium churn risk

### Business Interpretation
This segment has long-term value if nurtured correctly.

### Recommended Actions
- Upsell mid-tier plans
- Educational onboarding
- Usage-based incentives

---

## Conclusion
Customer segmentation enables targeted decision-making instead of a one-size-fits-all strategy.  
Each segment requires a distinct business approach to maximize retention and revenue.
